Disease: Powdery Mildew

Affects: Wide range of plants (Squash, Cucumber, Tomato, Rose)

Symptoms: White, flour-like circular spots on leaves, stems, and sometimes fruit. Infected leaves may yellow, curl, or become distorted. Photosynthesis is impaired, leading to reduced yields.

Causes: Various fungi (e.g., Erysiphe, Podosphaera). Unlike most fungi, it thrives in warm, dry climates with high relative humidity at night.

Treatment:
Apply sulfur-based fungicides or potassium bicarbonate
Use neem oil or horticultural oils for organic control
Milk-water sprays (40/60 ratio) can be effective in bright sunlight

Prevention: Choose resistant varieties. Plant in full sun. Improve air circulation. Avoid excessive nitrogen fertilization which creates succulent, susceptible growth.

Severity: LOW to MODERATE — usually manageable but can weaken plants over time.
