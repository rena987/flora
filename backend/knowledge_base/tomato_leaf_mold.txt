Disease: Tomato Leaf Mold

Affects: Tomato (primarily in greenhouses)

Symptoms: Pale green or yellow spots on the upper surface of older leaves. The undersides of these spots develop an olive-green to brown velvety mold (spores). Leaves eventually turn brown and die.

Causes: Fungus Passalora fulva. Spreads by wind and splashing water. Thrives in high humidity (>85%) and temperatures between 21-24°C.

Treatment:
Increase ventilation and air movement around plants
Apply fungicides like chlorothalonil or copper-based sprays
Remove and destroy heavily infected lower foliage

Prevention: Use resistant cultivars. Maintain humidity below 85% in greenhouses. Space plants widely. Avoid wetting leaves during irrigation.

Severity: MODERATE — primarily a concern in high-humidity environments or greenhouses.
