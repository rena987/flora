Disease: Tomato Late Blight

Affects: Tomato, Potato, Petunia

Symptoms: Water-soaked, dark green to lead-gray spots on leaves that expand rapidly into large brown lesions. White, fuzzy fungal growth often appears on the undersides of leaves in humid conditions. Stems develop dark brown streaks.

Causes: Oomycete pathogen Phytophthora infestans. Spreads rapidly through wind-borne sporangia. Favored by cool, moist nights (10-15°C) and moderate day temperatures (15-21°C).

Treatment:
Prune and destroy infected plants immediately; do not compost
Apply fungicides containing chlorothalonil, mancozeb, or fixed copper
Use biofungicides containing Bacillus subtilis for organic control
Ensure foliage stays dry by improving ventilation

Prevention: Plant resistant varieties. Avoid planting tomatoes near potatoes. Destroy volunteer plants and cull piles. Use drip irrigation.

Severity: CRITICAL — can destroy an entire crop within days under favorable conditions.
