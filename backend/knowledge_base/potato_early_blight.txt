Disease: Potato Early Blight

Affects: Potato, Tomato, Eggplant

Symptoms: Small, dark, dry papery spots on older leaves. As spots enlarge, they show characteristic concentric "bullseye" rings. Tubers may develop dark, sunken, circular lesions with corky tissue underneath.

Causes: Fungus Alternaria solani. Spreads via wind and water splash. Favored by alternating wet and dry periods.

Treatment:
Apply fungicides (chlorothalonil, mancozeb, or azoxystrobin)
Ensure plants have adequate nitrogen and potassium to maintain vigor
Harvest tubers only when vines are completely dead to reduce skin damage

Prevention: Practice 3-year crop rotation. Use certified disease-free seed tubers. Avoid overhead irrigation. Control volunteer potato plants.

Severity: MODERATE to HIGH — reduces tuber size and storage life.
