Disease: Tomato Target Spot

Affects: Tomato, Pepper, Papaya

Symptoms: Small, brown, water-soaked spots on leaves that expand and develop a "target" appearance with concentric rings. Unlike Early Blight, these spots are often more numerous and smaller. Fruit shows sunken, brown, circular lesions.

Causes: Fungus Corynespora cassiicola. Spreads by wind and splashing water. Favored by high humidity and long periods of leaf wetness.

Treatment:
Apply fungicides containing azoxystrobin or chlorothalonil
Prune lower branches to improve airflow
Maintain consistent soil moisture

Prevention: Use 3-year crop rotation. Remove crop residues immediately after harvest. Avoid overhead irrigation.

Severity: MODERATE to HIGH — can cause significant defoliation in warm, humid regions.
