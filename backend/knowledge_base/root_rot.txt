Disease: Root Rot

Affects: Nearly all plants, especially in poorly drained soil

Symptoms: Stunted growth, yellowing leaves, and wilting despite wet soil. Roots appear brown, black, or mushy and may have a foul odor. The outer "skin" of the root may easily slough off.

Causes: Pathogenic water molds (Phytophthora, Pythium) or fungi (Rhizoctonia, Fusarium). Favored by overwatering and poorly drained, compacted soil.

Treatment:
Improve soil drainage immediately
Reduce watering frequency
Apply soil drench fungicides (like mefenoxam) for high-value plants
If severe, remove the plant and solarize the soil

Prevention: Do not overwater. Use well-draining soil or raised beds. Clean containers thoroughly before reusing. Ensure pots have drainage holes.

Severity: HIGH — often fatal once symptoms are visible above ground.
