Disease: Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (TYLCV)

Affects: Tomato, Pepper, Tobacco, Weeds

Symptoms: Severe stunting of the plant. New leaves are significantly smaller, yellow (chlorotic), and curled upward/inward. Flowers may drop before fruit sets.

Causes: Begomovirus transmitted exclusively by the silverleaf whitefly (Bemisia tabaci). Not spread by touch or seeds.

Treatment:
No cure for the virus; infected plants must be removed and destroyed immediately
Control whitefly populations using insecticidal soaps or neem oil
Use reflective mulches to deter whiteflies

Prevention: Plant TYLCV-resistant varieties. Use fine mesh row covers to exclude whiteflies. Remove host weeds from the vicinity.

Severity: CRITICAL — can result in 100% yield loss if plants are infected at an early stage.
