Disease: Potato Late Blight

Affects: Potato, Tomato

Symptoms: Large, irregular, water-soaked lesions on leaves and stems. In moist weather, white fungal growth appears on leaf undersides. Tubers develop a dry, reddish-brown rot that extends into the flesh.

Causes: Oomycete Phytophthora infestans. Spreads via wind-borne spores. Thrives in cool, wet weather.

Treatment:
Kill vines immediately if infection is found to protect tubers
Apply systemic fungicides like metalaxyl or protective fungicides like copper
Remove and destroy infected tubers to prevent spread in storage

Prevention: Plant resistant varieties. Use certified seed. Eliminate "volunteer" potatoes and cull piles. Monitor weather conditions for "blight cast" warnings.

Severity: CRITICAL — historically the cause of the Irish Potato Famine; can destroy crops rapidly.
