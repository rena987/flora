Disease: Tomato Early Blight

Affects: Tomato, Potato, Eggplant

Symptoms: Dark brown to black lesions with concentric rings forming a bullseye pattern. Lesions typically appear on older lower leaves first. Surrounding tissue turns yellow. Severely affected leaves drop prematurely.

Causes: Fungal pathogen Alternaria solani. Spreads via wind and water splash. Favored by warm temperatures (24-29°C), high humidity, and wet foliage.

Treatment:
Remove and destroy all affected leaves immediately
Apply copper-based fungicide (copper hydroxide or copper sulfate) every 7-10 days
Alternatively use chlorothalonil or mancozeb fungicides
Avoid overhead irrigation — water at base of plant
Apply mulch to prevent soil splash onto lower leaves

Prevention: Rotate crops every 2-3 years. Use certified disease-free seed. Space plants adequately for airflow. Remove plant debris after harvest.

Severity: HIGH — can cause significant defoliation and yield loss if untreated. Act within 48 hours of first symptoms.
