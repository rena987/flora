Disease: Downy Mildew

Affects: Grapes, Cucurbits, Brassicas, Spinach

Symptoms: Angular, pale yellow spots on the upper leaf surface, often bounded by leaf veins. A fuzzy, gray-to-purple mold appears on the underside of the spots during humid weather.

Causes: Oomycetes (e.g., Peronospora, Pseudoperonospora). Spreads via wind and water. Requires free water on leaves for infection.

Treatment:
Apply copper-based fungicides or mancozeb
Use systemic fungicides like phosphorous acid (phosphonate)
Remove infected plant debris immediately

Prevention: Ensure wide plant spacing. Use drip irrigation. Select resistant cultivars. Water early in the day so foliage dries quickly.

Severity: HIGH — spreads rapidly in wet conditions and can cause total crop failure.
