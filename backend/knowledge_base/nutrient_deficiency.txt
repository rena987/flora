Disease: Nutrient Deficiency (General)

Affects: All plants

Symptoms: General yellowing (Nitrogen), purpling of lower leaves (Phosphorus), or yellowing of leaf edges (Potassium). Interveinal chlorosis (green veins, yellow tissue) often indicates Iron or Magnesium deficiency.

Causes: Lack of specific minerals in the soil, improper soil pH (which "locks" nutrients), or damaged root systems unable to take up nutrients.

Treatment:
Conduct a soil test to identify specific missing nutrients
Apply balanced fertilizer or specific amendments (e.g., chelated iron)
Adjust soil pH to the appropriate range for the specific plant

Prevention: Regularly add compost or organic matter to the soil. Use slow-release fertilizers. Maintain consistent watering to allow nutrient transport.

Severity: VARIABLE — can range from minor growth slowing to plant death depending on the specific nutrient missing.
