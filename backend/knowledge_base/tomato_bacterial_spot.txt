Disease: Tomato Bacterial Spot

Affects: Tomato, Pepper

Symptoms: Small, water-soaked, greasy-looking spots on leaves that turn brown and necrotic. Fruit develops small, raised, scab-like lesions (2-3mm) that may have a yellowish halo.

Causes: Bacteria in the genus Xanthomonas. Spread by rain splash and contaminated seeds. Favored by high humidity and warm temperatures with heavy rainfall.

Treatment:
Apply copper-based bactericides combined with mancozeb
Remove and destroy infected plant parts
Avoid working in the garden when plants are wet

Prevention: Use certified pathogen-free seeds and transplants. Practice a 2-year crop rotation. Use mulch to reduce soil splashing. Avoid overhead watering.

Severity: HIGH — severely impacts fruit marketability and plant vigor in warm, wet climates.
