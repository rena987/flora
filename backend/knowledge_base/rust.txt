Disease: Rust

Affects: Grains, Beans, Roses, Garlic, Corn

Symptoms: Small, raised, orange, yellow, or reddish-brown pustules on leaf undersides. Powdery spores rub off easily on fingers. Leaves may eventually turn yellow and drop.

Causes: Various fungi in the order Pucciniales. Spreads via wind-borne spores. Requires moisture on leaf surfaces to germinate.

Treatment:
Prune and destroy infected leaves
Apply fungicides containing sulfur, copper, or myclobutanil
Clean tools thoroughly between plants

Prevention: Avoid overhead watering. Space plants for good airflow. Remove alternative host plants (some rusts require two different plant species to complete their life cycle).

Severity: MODERATE — can reduce vigor and yield but rarely kills the entire plant immediately.
