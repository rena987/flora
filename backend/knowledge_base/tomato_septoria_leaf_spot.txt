Disease: Tomato Septoria Leaf Spot

Affects: Tomato and other Solanaceous plants

Symptoms: Small, circular spots (3-5mm) with dark brown borders and tan or gray centers. Tiny black specks (pycnidia) appear in the centers of spots. Starts on lower leaves and progresses upward.

Causes: Fungus Septoria lycopersici. Overwinters on plant debris and weeds. Spreads via splashing water, insects, and handling wet plants.

Treatment:
Remove infected lower leaves to reduce inoculum
Apply fungicides such as chlorothalonil, mancozeb, or copper-based products
Improve air circulation through staking and pruning
Keep foliage dry

Prevention: Control solanaceous weeds (like nightshade). Rotate crops on a 3-year cycle. Thoroughly clean garden tools after use.

Severity: MODERATE to HIGH — reduces fruit quality and yield through heavy defoliation.
